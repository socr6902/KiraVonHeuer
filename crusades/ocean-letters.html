Love Letters to the Ocean

Environmental, Spiritual — Ongoing since 2020

The ocean taught me to make. Not through direct instruction, but through years of watching—tides, currents, the way light moves through water. The repetition of waves. The patience of erosion. The violence of storms that reshape coastlines overnight.

This crusade is a practice of reciprocity. The ocean has given me forms, rhythms, a way of understanding time. In return, I commit to actions that honor that exchange:

  • 10% of all atelier revenue supports ocean conservation organizations
  • Annual beach clean-ups with local communities
  • Material choices that minimize marine pollution
  • Refusal of air freight for shipments
  • Partnership with ocean research institutes

But beyond policy, this is emotional work. The ocean is not a resource to be managed. It is kin. Teacher. Collaborator. Making objects inspired by water while contributing to its destruction would be a betrayal of the work itself.

Some objects in the collections emerge directly from this crusade—the Wave Forms, the Ceremonial Vessels, certain pieces in the Amulets series. But the crusade is larger than any single object. It's the reason the atelier exists.

Why "Love Letters"?

Because this is not academic. Not dispassionate research. Not neutral observation. This is devotion. The kind that demands daily commitment, that shapes all decisions, that cannot be separated from the life that contains it.

A love letter is vulnerable. It confesses need. It says: you have changed me, and I am better for it. The ocean has changed me. Everything I make is evidence of that transformation.
