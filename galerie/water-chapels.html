Water Chapels

Galerie Marian Goodman, Paris
March 12 – May 18, 2024

Seven chambers, each holding a different relationship to water. Not as symbol but as presence—as sound, as reflection, as boundary between states.

Visitors moved through spaces where water determined the architecture. A room where rainfall was amplified through copper vessels. A passage flooded ankle-deep, requiring barefoot crossing. A chapel of glass containing only mist.

The work emerged from years observing tidal patterns, baptismal rites, and the way water holds light. These were not installations to look at but environments to enter—spaces that asked for slowness, for listening, for the willingness to be changed by passage.

Some works from this exhibition later evolved into the Ceremonial Vessels collection. But the spatial work remains distinct—ephemeral, un-ownable, existing only in the documentation and the memory of those who moved through.
